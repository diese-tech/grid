# Audio Atmosphere

## Purpose

This doc defines how sound should behave inside The Grid. Audio should quietly support immersion, atmosphere, and environmental memory without becoming annoying or overwhelming.

---

# Q/A Summary

1. Audio should be softly enabled by default when possible, with an obvious mute option.

2. The Grid should contain soft synth ambience, occasional server hum, fluorescent buzz, menu ticks, distant machinery, radio static, elevator hum, mechanical doors, and subtle water drops in liminal spaces.

3. The environment should feel empty but not alone.

4. Rare absurd humor moments are allowed, including occasional loud airhorn gags.

5. Audio should support the feeling of remembered spaces and operational atmosphere.

---

# Audio Philosophy

Sound should feel:

- environmental
- restrained
- atmospheric
- spatial
- operational
- slightly uncanny

The Grid should never feel silent, but it also should never feel noisy.

---

# Ambient Audio

Ambient layers can include:

- distant machinery
- server hum
- low synth ambience
- ventilation systems
- fluorescent buzz
- soft operational tones
- faint static
- environmental resonance

These sounds should feel subtle and layered.

---

# Liminal Space Audio

Construction zones, inactive sectors, archives, and rough spaces can contain:

- subtle water drops
- distant metallic echoes
- electrical flickers
- operational instability
- environmental emptiness

The emotional target:

> empty but not abandoned

---

# Interaction Audio

Interactions can include:

- menu ticks
- soft confirmations
- operational pulses
- subtle activation sounds
- node-selection feedback

Interaction audio should feel clean and responsive.

Avoid:

- loud arcade sounds
- exaggerated UI effects
- excessive notifications

---

# Humor Layer

The Grid is allowed to break tension occasionally.

Rare absurd moments can exist.

Example:

- an occasional random airhorn blast

These moments should feel:

- surprising
- human
- slightly chaotic
- rare enough to stay funny

---

# Audio Hierarchy

## Continuous

- ambience
- hums
- environmental systems
- low atmosphere

## Responsive

- interactions
- panel activation
- node selection
- transitions

## Rare Events

- joke sounds
- unstable system alerts
- unusual discoveries

---

# Audio Guardrail

If the sound design becomes distracting, constant, or theatrical, it is wrong.

The correct feeling is:

> remembered atmosphere running quietly beneath the interface
