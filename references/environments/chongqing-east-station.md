## Source

Chongqing East Railway Station reference.

## Why It Belongs

This reference clarifies the homepage/transit-layer direction better than generic sci-fi station imagery. It grounds The Grid in believable near-future infrastructure instead of cyberpunk decoration.

The key value is not literal train-station theming.

The key value is:

> massive readable infrastructure that feels alive, connected, and operational.

## What To Steal Emotionally

- huge spatial scale
- clean transit logic
- layered architectural depth
- restrained futurism
- negative space
- movement implied through infrastructure
- operational calm
- believable public-system design
- soft lighting over excessive neon
- station-as-interchange feeling

## What NOT To Copy

- literal railway branding
- real-world signage systems too directly
- sterile airport/mall energy
- over-polished civic architecture with no personality
- turning The Grid into a literal train-station site

## Design Implications For The Grid

The homepage should feel less like a menu and more like an interchange.

This means:

- larger scale
- cleaner composition
- less UI clutter
- more architectural wayfinding
- subtle route signage
- more negative space
- stronger environmental depth
- believable infrastructure instead of sci-fi cosplay

The transit layer should imply that sectors connect through a real system.
